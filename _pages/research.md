## Exceptional points and non-Hermitian dynamics

This direction investigates how exceptional points and non-Hermitian spectral structures generate qualitatively new forms of quantum response, dynamics, and control. Particular emphasis is placed on the relationship between spectral singularities, dynamical slowing or speeding, and enhanced cooperative effects.

## Open quantum systems and quantum information

This direction studies dissipative and non-Markovian quantum dynamics with an emphasis on information-theoretic functionality. Topics include entanglement generation, quantum sensing, spectral signatures of environment-assisted behavior, and the interplay between coherence and dissipation.

## Floquet engineering and topological quantum matter

This direction explores driven quantum systems, engineered phases, and topological quantum phenomena. It includes Floquet routes to long-range superconducting correlations, topological interference, and dynamical control beyond standard high-frequency approximations.

## Quantum transport and Majorana-related phenomena

This direction develops theoretical models for hybrid quantum devices, including transport signatures connected with Majorana physics, superconducting structures, and coupled-dot architectures.

## Toward scalable quantum technologies

A longer-term direction is to connect fundamental theory with scalable quantum information platforms, including silicon-based quantum computation, engineered dissipative control, and advanced routes to quantum simulation.
