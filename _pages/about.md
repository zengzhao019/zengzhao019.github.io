Zengzhao Li is an Associate Researcher at Shenzhen International Quantum Academy. He works in theoretical quantum physics, with research interests spanning non-Hermitian quantum systems, exceptional-point dynamics, open quantum systems, quantum information, Floquet engineering, and quantum transport.

His research focuses on how spectral singularities, dissipation, and driven quantum dynamics can be harnessed for entanglement generation, energy transfer, quantum control, and scalable quantum technologies.

## Background

## Selected Research Themes

### Exceptional points and non-Hermitian dynamics
Understanding how exceptional points reshape spectral response, dynamical time scales, and cooperative effects in quantum systems.

### Open-system quantum dynamics
Studying dissipative and non-Markovian processes with emphasis on entanglement generation, transport, and spectroscopic signatures.

### Quantum information and control
Exploring control strategies and state-manipulation mechanisms emerging from non-Hermitian and driven quantum dynamics.

### Floquet and device-oriented theory
Developing theoretical routes toward engineered topological phases, Majorana-related physics, and scalable quantum hardware platforms.
