Zengzhao Li

Associate Researcher at Shenzhen International Quantum Academy, Shenzhen, China. My research develops a unified theoretical framework connecting non-Hermitian spectral structures, open quantum system dynamics, and quantum information processing, with emphasis on exceptional-point physics and quantum control mechanisms.

I focus on uncovering fundamental mechanisms for enhanced quantum sensing, entanglement generation, and controllable dynamics, bridging theory with emerging quantum technologies such as silicon-based quantum computing and quantum simulation.

Research

Core themes of my current work.

Exceptional points and non-Hermitian dynamics

Understanding how spectral singularities and non-Hermitian effects generate new mechanisms for quantum dynamics and control.

Open quantum systems and quantum information

Studying dissipative and non-Markovian dynamics with emphasis on sensing, entanglement generation, and information processing.

Floquet engineering, transport, and quantum devices

Exploring driven quantum phases, Majorana-related phenomena, and theoretical routes toward scalable quantum platforms.
