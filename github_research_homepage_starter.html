About

I am a theoretical researcher in quantum physics with broad interests spanning quantum simulation, quantum information and computation, open quantum systems, non-Hermitian physics, and quantum transport. My recent work has explored exceptional-point-enhanced entanglement generation, vibrationally assisted energy transfer, and theoretical mechanisms connecting spectral structures with quantum dynamical behavior.

Before my current position, I worked as an Assistant Project Scientist at the University of California, Berkeley. I received my PhD in theoretical physics from Fudan University in 2012. Across different projects, I have worked on analytical and numerical problems involving non-equilibrium dynamics, Lindblad evolution, non-Markovian effects, Floquet engineering, Majorana physics, and quantum-device theory.

Research Interests

  • Non-Hermitian quantum physics and exceptional points: Spectral topology, critical dynamics, and quantum-information-relevant effects near exceptional points.
  • Open quantum systems: Lindblad dynamics, non-Markovian behavior, decoherence, and environment-assisted quantum processes.
  • Quantum information and control: Quantum sensing, entanglement generation, quantum state manipulation, and gate-related control mechanisms.
  • Quantum simulation and mesoscopic theory: Majorana physics, quantum transport, Floquet engineering, and related solid-state quantum systems.

Selected Projects

  • Exceptional-point-enhanced quantum dynamics
    I study how higher-order and conventional exceptional points reshape quantum evolution, with particular interest in accelerated entanglement generation, enhanced response, and dynamical control mechanisms relevant to quantum technologies.
  • Vibrationally assisted energy transfer and spectroscopy
    My work investigates how structured vibrations and environments influence excitation-energy transfer, spectral signatures, and transport efficiency in molecular and aggregate systems.
  • Open-system and mesoscopic quantum theory
    I also work on non-equilibrium dynamics, quantum transport, Majorana-related phenomena, and theoretical models that connect condensed-matter platforms with broader questions in quantum information science.
